Reasons for Occurrence and Symptoms of Cervical Cancer

Cervical cancer can often be found early and sometimes even prevented by having regular screening tests.

Almost all cervical cancer cases are linked to infection with high-risk human papillomaviruses (HPV), and effective primary (HPV vaccination) and secondary prevention approaches will prevent most cervical cancer cases.

Symptoms of cervical cancer include vaginal discharge, abnormal vaginal bleeding, vaginal odor, and pain. It's important to see a doctor if experiencing any of these symptoms.
